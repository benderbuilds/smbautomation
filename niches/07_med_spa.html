Where your revenue is leaking

Med spa owners are running solid businesses with a structural gap: the same person responsible for delivering services is also responsible for lead follow-up and client retention. Something always gets deprioritized — and it's usually marketing.

  • Ad leads expire fast. A woman who clicked your Botox ad on Tuesday and didn't hear back by Wednesday has already moved on. The follow-up window is 24–48 hours max.
  • First-visit clients don't rebook without a prompt. Most clients intend to come back — they just don't think about it until they see a competitor's ad. A timely rebooking message at 55 days captures that intent.
  • Reviews aren't being systematically collected. One extra Google review per week compounds into a 50-review advantage over a competitor in 12 months. Almost no spa has an automated ask.
  • Lapsed clients are the easiest win. A client who last visited 6 months ago already trusts you. A single reactivation message typically converts 15–20% of that list.
