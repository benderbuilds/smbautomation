Where immigration practices lose revenue

Immigration attorneys have one of the strongest demand profiles in legal services. High emotional urgency, clear need, and clients with real ability to pay. The conversion problem is almost always operational — not competitive.

  • Intake happens at inconvenient times. Prospects fill out forms during their lunch break, at night, or on weekends — when attorneys are in court or unavailable. Those leads go cold by morning.
  • Case type triage is missing. An asylum case and a corporate H-1B have completely different urgency levels. Without a triage system, every lead gets the same delayed response.
  • No consultation pipeline. Most firms either follow up once and give up, or have an admin manually chasing every inquiry. Neither scales.
  • Referral partners aren't being nurtured. Notaries, real estate agents, and ethnic community organizations are your highest-quality referral sources. Almost no firm has a system to stay in front of them.
