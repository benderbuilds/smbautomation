Why most HRT clinics lose the conversion

Women's hormone clinics are growing faster than most providers can staff for. The demand is real — the conversion problem is almost always in what happens between the first inquiry and the booked consult.

  • The research window is long. Women spend 60–90 days evaluating options. Clinics that follow up once and move on lose the conversion to whoever stayed present.
  • Education converts more than promotion. The clinics winning in this niche are sending symptom explainers, treatment option comparisons, and what-to-expect guides — not sales emails.
  • Referral relationships need maintenance. OBGYNs and functional medicine practitioners are the highest-quality referral source. Most clinics don't have a system to nurture those relationships.
  • Reactivation is untapped. Patients who paused HRT — cost, life change, moved — are among the easiest re-enrollments. No one is following up with them.
