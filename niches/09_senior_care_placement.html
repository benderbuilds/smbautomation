Why families choose a different advisor

Senior care placement advisors provide an extraordinary service — but most lose placements not because of their knowledge or facility relationships, but because they go quiet during the most critical part of the decision process.

  • Families get overwhelmed and go dark. An aging parent crisis triggers a 1–3 week research frenzy followed by paralysis. The advisor who sends relevant, helpful content during that window stays in the decision.
  • One follow-up isn't enough. Most advisors reach out once or twice after the initial call. Families making this decision need 5–7 touches of useful information before they're ready to move forward.
  • Referral sources need maintenance. Hospital social workers, eldercare attorneys, and financial planners are your best referral sources — and they refer to whoever stays in front of them. Most advisors don't have a system for that.
  • Post-placement follow-up builds referrals. A family who placed a parent successfully will refer 2–3 other families if asked at the right time. Almost no advisor captures that systematically.
What we build for your practice
Family nurture sequence (1–3 week window)
A multi-touch educational sequence that sends facility touring checklists, memory care comparisons, and family conversation guides. Helpful — not salesy. Keeps you present through the decision.
Referral source nurture (quarterly)
Automated quarterly check-ins with social workers, eldercare attorneys, and financial planners who've referred in the past. A clinical resource, a case summary, a thank-you — keeps you top of their referral list.
Post-placement referral capture
A 30-day and 90-day post-placement check-in that confirms family satisfaction, collects a testimonial, and gently asks for referrals at the moment of highest goodwill.
Lapsed inquiry reactivation
Families who inquired 60–180 days ago and didn't place often return when the situation changes. A reactivation sequence puts you back in front of them before they call a different advisor.
