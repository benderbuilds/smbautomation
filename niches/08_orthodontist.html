The three revenue gaps most practices ignore

Orthodontic practices invest heavily in new patient marketing but rarely look at the revenue already in their system. Three gaps account for the majority of recoverable revenue in most practices.

  • No-shows. Each missed appointment slot costs $400–$800 in lost production time. A multi-touch reminder sequence — not just a single day-before text — typically cuts no-show rates by 30–40%.
  • Unconverted consultations. The average practice converts 40–50% of consultations to starts. The other half aren't gone — they're undecided. A 4-week post-consult nurture sequence recovers a meaningful portion.
  • Lapsed referral relationships. GPs who referred patients 12–18 months ago have since referred to someone else — not because they stopped liking you, but because they forgot about you. A quarterly check-in sequence fixes that.
  • No reactivation program. Patients whose treatment plans stalled or who moved mid-treatment represent real recoverable revenue. Almost no practice follows up systematically.
