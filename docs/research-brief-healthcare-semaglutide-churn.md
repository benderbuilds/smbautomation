# Research Brief: Reducing Patient Churn in Semaglutide/GLP-1 Programs

## Core Pain Point

Semaglutide subscription programs lose 40–60% of patients within the first 6 months, primarily during the titration phase (weeks 4–12) when side effects peak and results haven't yet materialized. Each lost patient represents $150–$500/month in recurring revenue. For a program with 200 active patients at $299/month, a 50% churn rate means ~$179,400/year in lost revenue from patients who already converted.

The business owner's problem: they know patients are dropping off, but their staff can't manually check in with every patient at the right moment. By the time someone notices a patient hasn't refilled, it's too late — they've already cancelled or ghosted.

## Supporting Statistics

- The GLP-1 medication market reached $24B+ in 2024 and is projected to exceed $100B by 2030 (Goldman Sachs, 2024)
- Approximately 50% of patients discontinue GLP-1 medications within the first year (Truveta Research, 2024 — based on real-world EHR data)
- The most common discontinuation window is weeks 4–12 during dose titration, when GI side effects are most severe (estimated, based on clinical trial attrition patterns)
- Customer acquisition cost for telehealth subscription patients ranges from $150–$400 per patient (estimated, based on industry benchmarks for DTC telehealth marketing)
- Retaining an existing customer costs 5–7x less than acquiring a new one (Harvard Business Review)

## Automation Angle

**Automated titration check-in sequences:** A multi-step SMS/email sequence triggered by the patient's dosage schedule. At key intervals (day 3, day 7, day 14 after each dose increase), the system sends a brief check-in asking about side effects, adherence, and satisfaction on a 1–5 scale.

- **Score 4–5:** Patient gets encouragement + educational content about what to expect next
- **Score 1–3:** Triggers an immediate alert to clinical staff for a personal outreach call within 24 hours
- **No response after 48 hours:** Escalation sequence begins (the silence is the danger signal)

Additional automations:
- **Pre-titration education drip:** Automated messages preparing patients for common side effects BEFORE they hit, reducing surprise-driven dropout
- **Win-back sequences:** For patients who miss a refill or cancel, a 30/60/90-day re-engagement campaign with updated pricing or program options
- **NPS/satisfaction surveys at day 30, 60, 90** feeding a dashboard the business owner can review weekly

The reader is a clinic owner or program operator staring at a spreadsheet of cancellations, not a patient Googling weight loss tips.

Frame it as: "Your marketing budget brings patients in the door. Your automation stack determines whether they stay."
