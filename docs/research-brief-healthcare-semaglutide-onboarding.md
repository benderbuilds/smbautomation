# Research Brief: Automating Semaglutide Patient Onboarding

## Core Pain Point

Onboarding a new semaglutide patient involves 6–10 discrete steps: intake form, medical history review, insurance/payment setup, lab order, lab results review, provider consultation, prescription, pharmacy coordination, and first-dose education. Most telehealth programs handle this with a patchwork of manual emails, phone calls, and portal messages — resulting in a 7–14 day onboarding window where patients frequently drop off before ever receiving their first dose.

The business owner's problem: they're paying $150–$400 to acquire each lead, but 20–30% of leads who complete an intake form never convert to a paying patient because the onboarding process is too slow, confusing, or requires too many manual touchpoints from staff.

## Supporting Statistics

- Average telehealth patient onboarding takes 7–14 days when managed manually (estimated, based on industry reports from telehealth operators)
- 20–30% of qualified leads drop off during onboarding before receiving their first prescription (estimated, based on telehealth conversion funnel benchmarks)
- For a program acquiring 50 new leads/month at $250 CAC, a 25% onboarding drop-off wastes ~$3,125/month in marketing spend ($37,500/year) (calculated)
- 67% of patients expect digital-first communication from healthcare providers (Accenture Health Consumer Survey, 2024)
- Telehealth visits grew 38x from pre-pandemic baselines and have stabilized at 13–17% of outpatient visits (McKinsey, 2024)

## Automation Angle

**End-to-end onboarding workflow automation:**

1. **Intake form submission** triggers immediate confirmation SMS + next-step instructions (no waiting for staff to manually respond)
2. **Automated lab order:** System generates and sends lab requisition to patient's preferred lab location within minutes of intake completion
3. **Lab results watcher:** Integration monitors for incoming lab results; when received, auto-routes to provider queue with pre-populated review template
4. **Provider review → prescription:** After provider signs off, system auto-generates prescription and coordinates with pharmacy
5. **Payment capture:** Automated billing setup with subscription enrollment before first shipment
6. **First-dose education sequence:** Day-of-shipment automated video/text series covering injection technique, storage, side effect management

**Key metric to track:** Time from intake form to first dose delivered. Industry average is 10–14 days. Automated programs report 3–5 days.

Additional automations:
- **Abandoned intake recovery:** If a lead starts but doesn't complete the intake form, a follow-up SMS within 2 hours ("We noticed you started your application — need help finishing up?")
- **Document collection bot:** Automated requests for ID verification, insurance cards, or consent forms with reminders until complete
- **Staff dashboard:** Real-time view of where every new patient is in the pipeline, with bottleneck alerts

## Differentiating Angle vs. Generic Health Content

This is NOT about "how to start a semaglutide clinic" or medical guidance. This is about **conversion rate optimization for patient onboarding** — treating the intake-to-first-dose pipeline like a sales funnel that can be measured, optimized, and automated. The reader is losing money to a leaky onboarding process and doesn't realize how much.

Frame it as: "Every day between intake and first dose is a day your new patient might choose a competitor instead."
