# Research Brief: Automating Symptom Tracking and Provider Alerts for HRT Programs

## Core Pain Point

Hormone replacement therapy (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone for women) requires ongoing symptom monitoring to dial in the right protocol. Patients experience fluctuating symptoms — hot flashes, mood changes, sleep disruption, libido shifts — that need to be tracked and reported to providers for dosage adjustments. Most HRT programs rely on patients to self-report during scheduled appointments, which means providers are making dosage decisions based on incomplete, recalled, and sometimes inaccurate information.

The business owner's problem: their providers are spending 15–20 minutes per follow-up appointment re-gathering symptom history that should have been collected continuously. Meanwhile, patients who are having a bad week between appointments have no structured way to flag it — they either suffer in silence and churn, or call/message the clinic creating unscheduled work for staff. There's no system that captures data continuously and surfaces only the patients who need attention.

## Automation Angle

**Automated symptom tracking and alert system:**

1. **Weekly symptom check-in (SMS/app):** Every [configurable day], patient receives a brief 2-minute survey:
   - Rate current symptoms on 1–10 scale (hot flashes, sleep, mood, energy, libido)
   - Any new symptoms? (yes/no, with free-text if yes)
   - Current medication adherence? (taken as prescribed / missed doses)
   - Overall satisfaction with treatment: 1–5

2. **Intelligent alert routing:**
   - **Green (stable):** Scores within expected range → data logged, no action needed
   - **Yellow (watch):** 2+ symptoms worsen by 3+ points week-over-week → flagged for provider review at next scheduled appointment
   - **Red (urgent):** Severe symptom spike, new concerning symptom, or 3+ consecutive declining weeks → immediate alert to provider for outreach within 24–48 hours

3. **Provider dashboard:**
   - Visual trend lines for each patient's symptom scores over time
   - Pre-populated appointment notes with symptom data since last visit
   - Sorted by urgency: red patients at top, green at bottom
   - Saves 10–15 minutes per follow-up appointment in history-gathering

4. **Patient engagement loop:**
   - Patients who complete check-ins get educational content matched to their top symptoms
   - Non-responders get a gentle nudge after 48 hours
   - 3 consecutive weeks of no response → staff alert (disengagement = churn risk)

**Key metrics:**
- Weekly check-in completion rate (target: 70%+)
- Average provider time per follow-up (before/after)
- Red alerts caught between appointments that would have been missed
- Patient retention rate at 6 and 12 months

## Differentiating Angle vs. Generic Health Content

This is NOT about "HRT symptom management tips" or "what to expect during hormone therapy." This is about **building a data pipeline between appointments** that makes the clinical team more efficient and the patient experience more responsive — without adding staff. The reader is an HRT program operator whose providers are overbooked and whose patients feel ignored between visits.

Frame it as: "Your providers shouldn't be spending appointment time asking 'how have you been feeling?' They should already know."
